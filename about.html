About the Practice

Where speech becomes the cure through its own unfolding

David M. Freshwater, LMHC

I bring eight years of experience practicing Lacanian psychoanalysis. My approach draws from the understanding that the unconscious operates according to its own sophisticated logic—one that often makes more sense than our conscious attempts to manage our lives.

I work exclusively with adults who are ready for genuine change, not just symptom management. This isn't therapy focused on coping strategies, but an investigation that can fundamentally transform how you experience yourself and your world.

I came to this work through my own transformative experience of analysis, which continues to inform my practice. The courage it takes to begin this journey is something I deeply respect in each person who reaches out.

All sessions are conducted online via secure video or phone connection, allowing for flexibility while maintaining the intimacy necessary for analytic work. My practice is small and intentionally limited, focusing on ongoing psychoanalytic relationships.

My Approach to Practice

Unlike therapists who offer techniques and strategies, I create conditions where your unconscious can emerge. This requires a particular kind of listening—one attuned to the gaps, repetitions, and surprises in your speech.

Each person's analysis unfolds in its own time and rhythm. What distinguishes this work is the space it creates—a space where what you've kept at bay can finally be spoken, where patterns reveal their logic, and where the unconscious coordinates of your desire become clearer. The transformations that emerge aren't imposed from outside but arise from your own speech as you begin to hear yourself differently.

My commitment is to the process of analysis itself—to maintaining the frame within which transformation becomes possible. This means respecting the power of the unconscious and trusting in its capacity to guide us toward what needs to be known.

Your First Step

We begin with an initial consultation where you'll have the opportunity to speak about what brings you to seek analysis. This isn't an intake or evaluation—it's a chance for both of us to get a sense of whether this particular approach aligns with what you're seeking.

During this meeting, I'll listen in the particular way that characterizes analytic work, and you'll begin to experience what it might be like to work together.

Working Together

My practice operates on Lacanian principles, which means sessions are tailored to the natural rhythms of analytic work rather than predetermined timeframes.

I work with a limited number of analysands to ensure each person receives the focused attention this work requires. My practice is fully online, serving adults throughout Massachusetts who are ready to engage deeply with their unconscious material.

Practical Considerations

This work requires a genuine commitment to the process. Most analysands find that meeting multiple times per week allows the work to deepen more effectively, though we'll determine frequency based on your particular situation.

Unlike quick-fix approaches, analysis is a journey that unfolds over time. The changes that emerge tend to be profound and lasting, affecting not just symptoms but your fundamental relationship to satisfaction and desire.
