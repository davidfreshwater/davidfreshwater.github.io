A Different Kind of Therapy

I practice Lacanian psychoanalysis—an approach where transformation happens through your own words, as new meanings emerge and old patterns dissolve.

Unlike traditional therapy focused on coping strategies, Lacanian analysis investigates the unconscious logic behind your symptoms and repetitions. Your unconscious communicates through symptoms, bungled actions, dreams, and slips of the tongue. I create conditions where you can begin to hear what you're trying to tell yourself.

Why People Seek Analysis

Each person's reasons are unique, but common threads include:

Finding yourself in familiar situations with different people
Navigating challenging relationships and seeking clarity
Persistent depression or anxiety that colors each day
Past traumas haunting and impacting your present life
Physical symptoms that defy medical explanation
Questioning your relationship with substances, porn, or social media
Living a life that looks good on paper but feels empty
Constantly meeting others' needs while losing your own
Success inevitably followed by mysterious self-sabotage

Perhaps you recognize yourself here, or your reasons remain uniquely your own.
There is no "correct" reason to seek analysis—your path is singular, your motivations legitimate.
