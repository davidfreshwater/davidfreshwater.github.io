Understanding the Process

You call your new partner by your ex's name at dinner with friends. You can't remember your sister's partner's name after two years. Stomach pain appears before every family gathering. You spill red wine on your mother-in-law's white carpet—for the second time. Every July, depression arrives like clockwork, though nothing happened in July that you can recall. You dream of speeding down a busy city street, but the brakes don't work.

These aren't random events—they're your psyche's way of insisting on being heard.

Speaking Freely

Psychoanalysis consists in speaking freely, without censoring the ideas that go through your head. I listen from a position that allows something unexpected to emerge. The power comes from discovering that the saying itself—when addressed to someone who listens from a certain position—can replace repetitive and predictable symptoms.

Unlike approaches that aim to strengthen the ego or provide coping strategies, Lacanian analysis works to reveal the unconscious structures that organize your life.

We return again and again to certain phrases, forgotten moments, seemingly trivial details—a recurring number, a childhood smell, a phrase you can't stop saying—until they lose their power and genuine change becomes possible.

How Sessions Work

Free Association

You speak whatever comes to mind without censoring. Dreams, memories, current events, seemingly random thoughts—all are welcome. What seems irrelevant often proves most revealing.

Precise Listening

I listen for patterns, repetitions, slips of the tongue, and the gaps in your speech. What you don't say often matters as much as what you do. A single word might become the key to everything.

Variable Session Length

Unlike traditional 50-minute hours, Lacanian sessions end when something essential has been articulated. This might be 20 minutes or 90—the unconscious doesn't follow clock time.

Creating Space for Discovery

Rather than offering solutions or providing answers, I respond in ways that help you hear your own words differently—allowing meanings you didn't know were there to surface.

What to Expect in Sessions

Analysis typically begins with a sense of urgency—something isn't working, familiar situations keep recurring, or symptoms have become unbearable.

You'll be invited to speak freely—to follow your associations wherever they lead. This might feel unusual at first, especially if you're used to more structured conversations.

I'll intervene at specific moments—sometimes with a question, sometimes by highlighting a word or phrase you've used, sometimes by punctuating the session when something essential has been articulated. These interventions are designed to open new avenues for exploration.

There's no "right" way to do analysis. Some people talk about dreams, others about daily life, others about memories or fantasies. What matters is that you speak as freely as possible and remain curious about what emerges.

"Little by little, from within your own words, another meaning forms and surprises you, then falls apart, taking the pain with it."

— Jacques-Alain Miller

The Process of Transformation

Initially, anxiety may increase as we approach what your psyche has kept hidden. This is actually a positive sign—it means we're getting close to material that matters. Many find that what seemed like setbacks were actually breakthroughs in disguise.

Over time, you may experience:

  • Dreams becoming more vivid and meaningful
  • Unexpected connections between disparate parts of your life
  • A shift in how you experience your symptoms
  • The profound relief of finally being heard in a way that allows something to change
  • The surprising lightness that comes when long-carried burdens finally find words

The unconscious resists being known directly. It reveals itself through dreams, symptoms, jokes, and errors. Our work is to create conditions where these communications can be heard and transformed.

The Analytic Relationship

This isn't a relationship of equals sharing experiences. I occupy a specific position that allows your unconscious to emerge. You'll likely develop strong feelings about me—positive and negative. These transference feelings become crucial material for understanding your patterns with others.

Sessions are conducted online via secure video or phone connection. The online format has proven remarkably effective for analysis, as the unconscious speaks just as clearly through digital connection as in person.

This depth of work asks for genuine commitment—temporal, financial, and emotional. Your investment reflects and enables the profound shifts possible through engaging with your own unconscious. Fees are discussed during our initial consultation, as the investment in analysis is an important part of the work itself.

Is This Approach Right for You?

Lacanian analysis is particularly effective for those who:

  • Sense that their problems have deeper roots than current circumstances
  • Find themselves in recurring situations despite understanding them intellectually
  • Are curious about their inner life and willing to be surprised
  • Seek fundamental change rather than temporary relief
  • Can tolerate uncertainty and the time needed for deep work

The Nature of Change

Some analyses last months, others years. The work continues until you've experienced sufficient change—until the repetitive cycles that brought you here have given way to new possibilities for living.

This approach serves those who are open to discovering something unexpected about themselves and who sense that authentic change is possible through patient engagement with the unconscious.

If this resonates with how you want to engage with your own psyche, we should explore working together.
