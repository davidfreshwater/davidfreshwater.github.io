What Makes This Approach Unique

Variable-Length Sessions

Sessions end at the logical moment dictated by the unconscious material, not by the clock. This respects the natural rhythm of analytic work and can produce powerful insights.

Focus on Your Speech

The emphasis is on what emerges through your own words. Slips of the tongue, repetitions, and contradictions all provide valuable information about unconscious processes.

No Predetermined Goals

Rather than working toward specific outcomes, we follow where your unconscious leads. This open-ended approach allows for discoveries that couldn't be anticipated.

The Position of Not-Knowing

I don't presume to know what's best for you. Instead, I help create conditions where your own truth can emerge and be recognized.

How This Differs from Traditional Therapy

Traditional Therapy: Often focuses on symptom reduction, coping strategies, and conscious understanding. Sessions are typically 50 minutes, with specific goals and treatment plans.

Lacanian Analysis: Investigates the unconscious structures organizing your life. Sessions vary in length, following the logic of the unconscious. The goal is transformation at the level of your fundamental relationship to desire and satisfaction.
