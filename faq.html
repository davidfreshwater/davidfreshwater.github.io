About the Process

How does this differ from other therapeutic approaches?

Most therapies treat symptoms as problems to eliminate. But psychoanalysis understands that symptoms can't be excised like tumors. When we try to simply remove them without addressing their unconscious roots, they resurface in new forms, mutating and returning like zombies.

Lacanian analysis takes a radically different path. Your symptoms aren't enemies to defeat but encrypted messages from your unconscious. Through free association—saying whatever comes to mind—you begin to decode what your unconscious has been trying to tell you all along. Sessions end at breakthrough moments rather than by the clock, and I listen for the patterns in your speech that reveal what you truly want beneath what you think you should want.

This work takes patience and courage, but offers genuine transformation. In analysis, symptoms often do resolve—sometimes dramatically—but not through outright attack. They dissolve when you discover what unconscious conflicts they were expressing. Unlike quick fixes, this changes your fundamental relationship to yourself and your desire.

What happens during sessions?

You speak freely about whatever comes to mind—dreams, memories, daily concerns, or seemingly random thoughts. I listen for patterns, repetitions, and contradictions in your speech. There's no agenda or exercises. I might offer interpretations that open new avenues, but the real work happens through your own process of articulation and discovery. Sessions end when something essential has emerged, not by the clock.

How is online analysis effective?

Analysis operates through speech and language—the unconscious reveals itself through words, dreams, and associations regardless of medium. The essential elements remain fully intact online: free association, careful listening, and the analytic frame.

Many analysands find online work allows deep engagement with added flexibility. Some even speak more freely from their own space. Physical presence isn't essential when the work happens through language itself.

Duration and Commitment

How long does analytic work typically continue?

There's no standard duration—some analyses last months, others years. The process concludes when repetitive patterns that brought you here have shifted into new possibilities. You'll sense when sufficient transformation has occurred.

This isn't about meeting external criteria but recognizing a fundamental change in how you experience yourself and your world. The work continues as long as it remains alive and productive for you.

How often are sessions?

Frequency varies based on what serves your process. Most meet weekly at a bare minimum, though some periods benefit from more intensive work while others call for spacing. We determine this together, following what the material demands rather than rigid rules.

Financial Considerations

Why private pay rather than insurance?

Insurance requires diagnoses, treatment goals, and measurable outcomes—antithetical to open-ended exploration. Private pay ensures:

  • Complete confidentiality without diagnoses in permanent records
  • Freedom to work at your unconscious's pace
  • No session limits or mandated treatment plans
  • Support for variable-length sessions
  • Deeper commitment through personal investment

While I can provide Superbills for out-of-network benefits, this requires a diagnosis—which some find contradicts the exploratory nature of analysis.

Is This Right for You?

How do I know if this approach is right for me?

This work suits those who can tolerate not-knowing, who are curious about their unconscious, and who sense deeper transformation is possible. If you need immediate symptom relief or concrete solutions, other approaches may serve you better.

Good candidates often feel they're stuck in a repeating cycle, "living someone else's life", or sense their symptoms carry hidden meaning. The consultation helps us explore whether this approach aligns with what you're seeking.

Do you work with specific issues or diagnoses?

I work with people, not diagnoses. Whatever brings you—relationship patterns, anxiety, depression, creative blocks, existential questions—what matters is your willingness to explore your unconscious. The form your suffering takes is less important than your curiosity about what it might mean.

Getting Started

What happens in the initial consultation?

The consultation lets you speak about what brings you and experience how we might work together. This isn't an intake or evaluation—it's a real encounter where you can speak freely while I listen analytically.

By the end, we'll both sense whether this approach fits your needs. The consultation stands alone with no obligation to continue.
