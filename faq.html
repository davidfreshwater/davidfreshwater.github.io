About the Process

How does this differ from other therapeutic approaches?

Most therapies aim to eliminate symptoms through changing thoughts, behaviors, or brain chemistry. Lacanian analysis recognizes symptoms as meaningful communications from your unconscious. Through speaking freely and being heard in a particular way, transformation happensâ€”not just understanding.

Unlike CBT or solution-focused approaches, we don't work toward predetermined goals. Instead, we follow where your unconscious leads, allowing for discoveries that neither of us can anticipate.

What happens during sessions?

You speak freely about whatever comes to mind while I listen for patterns, repetitions, and contradictions in your speech. There's no agenda except following where your associations lead. I might offer interpretations that open new avenues of exploration, but the real work happens in your own process of articulation and discovery.

Sessions end when something essential has been articulated, not by the clock. This respects the natural rhythm of the unconscious.

How is online analysis effective?

The essence of psychoanalysis is in speech and listening. Whether in person or online, the unconscious manifests through language, slips, dreams, and associations. Many analysands find that online work allows for deep engagement while offering practical flexibility.

The analytic frameâ€”regular sessions, consistent presence, focused attentionâ€”remains intact regardless of the medium.

Duration and Commitment

How long does analytic work typically continue?

There is no standard duration. Some analyses last months, others years. The process concludes when you've experienced sufficient transformationâ€”when the repetitive patterns that brought you here have given way to new possibilities. Analysis continues until you are sufficiently satisfied with the changes you've experienced.

This isn't about meeting external criteria but about your own sense of fundamental shift in how you experience yourself and your world.

How often are sessions?

Most people meet 1-2 times per week, though this can vary. Some periods of analysis benefit from more frequent meetings, while other times less frequency serves the work better. We determine this together based on what the material demands.

Can I take breaks or pause the work?

Life circumstances sometimes require pauses in analytic work. What matters is how these breaks are handledâ€”they often become important material for analysis itself. The unconscious continues working even during interruptions.

How is Lacanian analysis different from therapy?

Traditional therapy often focuses on symptom management, coping strategies, and conscious problem-solving. Lacanian analysis works differently:

  • We don't aim to eliminate symptoms but understand their unconscious logic
  • Sessions end at significant moments rather than by the clock
  • The analyst doesn't offer advice or interpretations but helps you hear your own unconscious
  • Change happens through your own discoveries rather than applied techniques
  • The goal is structural transformation, not just feeling better

This approach takes more time but creates lasting change at the level of your fundamental relationship to desire and enjoyment.

Is This Right for You?

How do I know if this approach is right for me?

This work serves those who can tolerate ambiguity, who are curious about their own mental processes, and who sense that genuine transformation is possible. If you're seeking immediate symptom relief or concrete problem-solving, other modalities would serve you better.

The 90-minute consultation helps determine if this approach aligns with what you're seeking. Good candidates often describe feeling like they're "living someone else's life" or sensing there's more to their symptoms than meets the eye.

What if I'm already in therapy?

Some people transition from therapy to analysis when they feel they've reached the limits of what their current approach can offer. Others may pause therapy to engage in analysis. This is something we can discuss during the consultation based on your specific situation.

Do you work with specific issues or diagnoses?

I don't organize my practice around diagnostic categories or specific symptoms. People seek analysis for various reasonsâ€”relationship patterns, anxiety, depression, creative blocks, existential questions. What matters is your willingness to engage with your unconscious material, not the particular form your suffering takes.
